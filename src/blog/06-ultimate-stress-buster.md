It's 7 PM, your inbox is overflowing, and your shoulders are tense from hours at your desk. You could scroll your phone or binge another show — or you could step into a boxing class and punch your stress away. More and more people are discovering that boxing classes are the ultimate way to shake off the day and recharge for tomorrow.

## Why boxing is such a powerful stress reliever
A 2022 review in the *Journal of Psychiatric Research* found that non-contact boxing exercises, especially in group settings, led to significant reductions in anxiety, depression and stress symptoms. It's the combination of high-intensity movement and mindful focus — you're channelling your energy, focusing on technique, and letting go of the day's frustrations with every punch.

## The numbers don't lie
A cross-sectional analysis of over 1.2 million Americans found those who engaged in boxing exercises reported a 20.1% lower mental health burden compared to those who didn't exercise at all. Beyond stress, boxing can also reduce anxiety and depression, improve sleep, and boost overall well-being.

## What happens in a class that melts stress

- A warm-up to get your body moving and your mind off work.
- Punching drills, bag work and footwork that demand your full attention.
- Short bursts of intense activity, followed by mindful recovery and deep breathing.
- A supportive group atmosphere where everyone's working toward the same goal.
