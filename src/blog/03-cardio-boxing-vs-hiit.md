Both are intense. Both burn calories. Both promise results in less time than a steady-state run. The honest question for most working professionals isn't which is "better" in a lab — it's which one you'll actually stick with twice a week for six months.

## What each one actually is
**HIIT** is a structure, not a movement. Short bursts of all-out work (20–40 seconds) followed by brief rest, repeated for 20–30 minutes. Exercises rotate — burpees, sprints, jump squats. Brutal, simple, no trainer required.

**Cardio boxing** is also built on intervals — but the work is punching combinations, footwork, and bag and pad drills. Three-minute rounds, 60-second rest. You're learning a skill while your heart rate stays in the same zones HIIT pushes.

## Where they're roughly the same

- **Calorie burn** — both put most adults in the 500–700 kcal range for a 45–60 minute session.
- **Cardio adaptation** — both improve VO2 max and resting heart rate within 4–6 weeks.
- **Fat loss** — both work, assuming nutrition is in order. Neither is magic.

## Where cardio boxing pulls ahead

- **Skill keeps you engaged** — every class teaches you something, so people show up more often.
- **Posture and core** — every punch is a rotational core movement. If you sit 8+ hours a day, that matters more than calories.
- **Stress release** — hitting a heavy bag does something burpees don't.
- **Lower injury rate** — coached classes are safer than unsupervised home HIIT.

## Where HIIT pulls ahead

- **No coach or gear needed** — do it in a hotel room or a small space at home.
- **Pure time efficiency** — 20 minutes can replicate most of the cardio benefit of a 60-minute class.
- **No learning curve** — start hard on day one, with no stance-and-footwork phase.

## The honest verdict
If you have 20 minutes, no coach and no equipment — do HIIT. It works. If you have 60 minutes and you're choosing what to commit to for the next six months, boxing for fitness wins on sustainability. Most people quit HIIT because it gets boring. Almost nobody quits boxing for that reason.
