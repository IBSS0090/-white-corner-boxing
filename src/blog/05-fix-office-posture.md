If you spend your days hunched over a laptop, you're not alone. Office workers know stiff shoulders, aching backs and that afternoon energy crash all too well. But what if you could fix your posture, boost your energy and get fit — all at once? Enter boxing classes.

## Why office workers need more than a walk
Sitting for hours wrecks your posture. Your shoulders round forward, your core weakens, your lower back complains. A quick walk helps, but often isn't enough to undo a sedentary day. Boxing is a full-body workout that targets the very muscles office workers neglect — every punch, slip and duck engages your core, opens your chest and strengthens your back.

## How boxing improves posture and energy

- **Activates your core** — strong abs and obliques support your spine and help you sit taller.
- **Strengthens your upper back** — punching and defence build the muscles that pull your shoulders back.
- **Improves flexibility** — mobility work loosens tight hips and hamstrings.

And the energy boost is real. Boxing is high-intensity, so it gets your heart pumping and your blood flowing. After a session, most people report feeling more alert, focused and ready to tackle the rest of their day.

## Tips for office workers starting out

- **Start slow** — look for beginner-friendly classes that focus on technique and form.
- **Be consistent** — two to three sessions a week to see real improvements.
- **Listen to your body** — tell your coach about aches so they can modify exercises.
- **Stay fuelled** — good nutrition and hydration speed recovery.

Step away from your desk and into the ring — your body (and your boss) will thank you.
